About Me

Built for this, in and out of the water.

I've been in the water since I was 5 years old, nearly 15 years of competitive swimming that took me from age group meets in Irvine all the way to USA Swimming National Championships and the Ivy League.

I'm currently a rising sophomore on the Columbia University men's swimming team, competing in the Ivy League while maintaining a 3.75 GPA. Growing up, I swam for the Irvine Novaquatics and captained the Northwood High School team, where we won the CIF State Championship my senior year. Along the way I earned 16 Winter Junior National cuts (SCY & LCM), 16 Summer Junior National cuts (SCY & LCM), and 6 USA Swimming National cuts, and achieved the Olympic Trials time standards in both the 200 free and 200 fly.

Coaching is something I genuinely love. I've worked with swimmers of all ages, from kids who are afraid to put their face in the water to competitive club swimmers chasing faster splits. I'm patient, positive, and I know how to break down technique in a way that actually clicks. My goal is simple: every swimmer leaves the lesson better than they arrived.

FAQ

Common questions.

Where are lessons held?
All lessons are held at Forest Glen Pool, 101 Forest Glen, Irvine, CA 92602. It's a great facility centrally located in Orange County and easy to get to from Irvine, Tustin, Lake Forest, Newport Beach, and surrounding cities.
What ages do you teach?
All ages, from young beginners (ages 4–5+) through adults. I've taught kids who are afraid of the water all the way up to high school competitive swimmers and adults learning for fitness or triathlons.
What skill levels do you work with?
All levels. Complete beginners, recreational swimmers, swim team athletes, and competitive club swimmers are all welcome. Every lesson is tailored to the individual, there's no one-size-fits-all approach.
How do I pay?
Payment is accepted via Venmo, Zelle, or cash. For single lessons, payment is due at the time of the lesson. Package deals are paid upfront in full to lock in the discount.
What's your cancellation policy?
Please give at least 24 hours notice to cancel or reschedule. Late cancellations may be subject to a fee. I get that life happens, just communicate early and it won't be an issue.
How quickly will my swimmer see improvement?
Most swimmers show noticeable improvement within their first 2–3 lessons. Beginners often gain real water confidence and basic stroke mechanics within 4–6 sessions. Competitive swimmers typically see measurable technique and time improvements within a few weeks of consistent lessons.
Do you offer sibling discounts?
Yes, if you're booking back-to-back lessons for multiple kids at the same pool, ask about a sibling rate when you reach out.
How do I book?
Text or email with your swimmer's age, current level, and goals. I'll get back to you quickly and send a booking link to lock in your time slot.
